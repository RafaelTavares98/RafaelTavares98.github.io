Case description

A food company wants to produce the highest profit for the next direct marketing campaign, scheduled for the next month.

A pilot campaign involving 2.240 customers was carried out, resulting in an 15% success rate (15% or 336 people said "Yes" and bought the offer while the other 85% or 1904 didn't)

The campaign had a total cost of 6.720 MU and gave in profit -3.406 MU. Resulting in

  • Cost per Contact (CPC) of 3,0 MU
  • Cost per Acquisition (CPA) of 20,0 MU
  • Revenue per Sale (Average) of 10,9 MU

The Problem: It costs the company 20 MU to "convince" someone to buy. But that person only spends 10,93 MU on average, therefore, the company is losing 9,07 per sale.

In order to improve this scenario, we'll analyse customer data and develop a Tableau dashboard to better describe the customer behavior.

To do that, we must cover 3 main objectives:

  • Which customers are the most profitable to target?
  • Which products are the most profitable to target?
  • Are "engaged" customers more profitable than "new" customers?
